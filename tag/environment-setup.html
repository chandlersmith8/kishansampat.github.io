Setting up your environment

Published: Mon 18 May 2020
By Kishan Sampat

In Learning Path.

tags: environment setup

I have a Windows 10 PC with 16GB of RAM and a 500GB SSD Hard Disk. I was keen to initally try setting up my environment in Windows, however, I faced a number of errors just trying to get Python installed.

While it is not impossible to get your environment set up on Windows, as a beginner, I decided to go with a VirtualBox setup and installed Ubuntu in it. My PC has enough processing power, and it was proving to be a significant overhead to try and get Python installed; I thought it was better to go with an Ubuntu setup as I also found instructions on the web much easier to follow for Ubuntu.

I installed VirtualBox and gave it about half of my RAM (around 8GB), and about 100 GB of hard drive space. While 2GB should be enough for the RAM, I decided to give it more power as I anticipate running heavy queries in the future. And with that, we’re good to kick off our learning!
